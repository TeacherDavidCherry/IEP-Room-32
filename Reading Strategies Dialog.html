Reading Strategies – Dialogue

Student: I don’t get it. How do I even start reading something new?

Teacher: If it feels overwhelming, calm down and focus on one small part at a time. Make it easy! Break sentences into smaller parts.

Student: Break them up? Why?

Teacher: It helps you focus. You don’t need to understand everything at once. Focus on one thing until you get it.

Student: So, how do I know where to break a sentence?

Teacher: Break it after commas or words like “and,” “but,” or “because.” It makes it easier to read.

Student: Alright, cool. What’s next?

Teacher: Next, look for context clues in the text. Words around the unknown word can help you guess its meaning.

Student: Oh, like “It's a sport” helps me understand at least a little bit if they are talking about a new sport that I have never heard of before.

Teacher: Exactly! And remember, Context clues are words, not pictures. Pictures can be clues, but when we say context clues, we mean words, not pictures. And remember that pictures aren’t always very good clues. Look at the words.

Student: Got it! What else?

Teacher: Use what you already know. If the text is about a sport you never heard of, and you know about other sports already, use that information to help understand this new sport better.

Student: So I can use my general knowledge of sport to understand a text about a new sport better?

Teacher: Yes! It’s making connections with what you already know.

Student: That helps a lot! But what if I still don’t get the main idea?

Teacher: That’s okay. Reread the section. Going over it again helps you understand better and catch things you missed.

Student: So, reread if I don’t get it?

Teacher: Exactly. And don’t forget, focus on the main idea, not every small detail. That’ll help you understand the text faster.

Student: Right! And what about those tough words when I cannot guess the meaning from the rest of the text?

Teacher: If you don’t understand a word, look it up. But try to guess first, then check with a dictionary if you’re still unsure.

Student: So, don’t stop and look up every word?

Teacher: That is correct! Guess first, then check to see if you were right. You should practice your other reading skills first instead of taking the easy way out and looking the new word up.

Student: Okay, so break it up, look for clues, use what I know, reread, focus on the main idea, and after I have practiced my other skills, look words up to see if I was correct! Got it!

Teacher: Exactly! You’re all set. Keep practicing, and reading will get easier each time.
